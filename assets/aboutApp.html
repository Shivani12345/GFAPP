About Human Trafficking

This is a platform for faith leaders.

A resource to help you use your uniquely powerful position.

To help stop a complicated crime which can have terrible social consequences: human trafficking.

There are many forms of this crime.
They all share basic characteristics:

When people - often the young, and often women - are traded or treated like foods. Under Ghanaian law, people who do this are criminals.

But these are often very difficult crimes to investigate and prosecute.

The police - which even have a specialist trafficking unit - don't have resources.

And trafficking crimes are often committed in a context of extreme poverty. This means the victims, or the family, become willing accomplices. With no money or food, they agree to abused financially or physically.

This GFN App is a dedicated practical and information tool for you: Faith Leaders, clergy, safeguarding staff etc. The purpose of this tool is to provide very clear and concise information about the crimes of modern slavery, information on how to respond to victims and methods to address the problem.

Know The Law

The Ghanaian law regulating these crimes is the 2005 Human Trafficking Act (2005).

The Act has a clear definition of human trafficking:

    the recruitment, transportation, transfer, harbouring, trading or receipt of persons for the purpose of exploitation within and across national borders by:

  • (a) The use of threats, force or other forms of coercion, abduction, fraud, deception, the abuse of power or exploitation of vulnerability, or
  • (b) Giving or receiving payments and benefits to achieve consent
